Table 8.0.1:: The Core Concept Model in Solution Evaluation

Core Concept

During Solution Evaluation, business

analysts...

Change

in response to a need. recommend a change to either a solution or the enterprise in order to realize the potential value of a solution.

Need be addressed. evaluate how a solution or solution component is fulfilling the need.

Solution

satisfying one or more needs in a context. assess the performance of the solution, examine if it is delivering the potential value, and analyze why value may not be realized by the solution or solution component.

Stakeholder

individual with a relationship to the change, the need, or the solution. elicit information from the stakeholders about solution performance and value delivery.

Value

usefulness of something to a stakeholder within a context. determine if the solution is delivering the potential value and examine why value may not be being realized.

Context

influence, are influenced by, and provide understanding of the change. consider the context in determining solution performance measures and any limitations within the context that may prohibit value from being realized.
