The Business Analysis Core Concept Model™

The

framework for business analysis. It encompasses what business analysis is and what it means to those performing business analysis tasks regardless of perspective, industry, methodology, or level in the organization. It is composed of six terms that have a common meaning to all business analysts and helps them discuss both business analysis and its relationships with common terminology.

Each of these terms is considered to be a core concept.

Table 2.1.1: The BACCM

Core Concept

Description

Change

The act of transformation in response to a need.

Change works to improve the performance of an enterprise.

These improvements are deliberate and controlled through business analysis activities.

Need A problem or opportunity to be addressed.

Needs can cause changes by motivating stakeholders to act.

Changes can also cause needs by eroding or enhancing the value delivered by existing solutions.

Solution

A specific way of satisfying one or more needs in a context.

A solution satisfies a need by resolving a problem faced by stakeholders or enabling stakeholders to take advantage of an opportunity.
