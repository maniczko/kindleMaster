4.5.3 Inputs Poor relationships with stakeholders can have many detrimental effects on business analysis, including: • failure to provide quality information, • strong negative reactions to setbacks and obstacles, • resistance to change, • lack of support for, and participation in, business analysis work, and • business analysis information being ignored.

These effects can be modified in part through strong, positive, and trust-based relationships with stakeholders. Business analysts actively manage relationships with stakeholders who: • provide services to the business analyst, including inputs to business analysis tasks and other support activities, • depend on services provided by the business analyst, including outputs of business analysis tasks, and • participate in the execution of business analysis tasks.
